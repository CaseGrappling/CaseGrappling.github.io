Why try grappling, gi or nogi?

There are three main reasons that someone would train grappling

  1. For self defense
    • Because grappling doesn’t injure your training partner, only controls them or puts them in a submission hold where they can “tap out” before they experience harm or injury, you can spar at 100% preparing yourself for the intensity of a real physical conflict without harming yourself or others.
    • Grappling exchanges are also extremely effective for fighting and in MMA wrestling / Jiu Jitsu is an integral part of training.
    • Weight and strength will always matter in any conflict, but in grappling they can be nullified with technique to a certain degree.
      • the best analogy for this I have heard about / seen is that a horse when standing is stronger than any man but when Mongolian herders need to tend to their horses or control them they will trip them down to their side and once on the ground a massive horse can be controlled by a single man.
  2. For exercise
    • To be a successful grappler in competition and in the practice room two things are needed to some degree (aside from technique). Luckily training can increase both of these attributes.
    • A famous quote in fighting is that “fatigue makes cowards of us all” and grappling is no different. A hard training will stress your cardiovascular system like nothing else.
    • Training regularly will increase your cardio and burn more calories than almost any other form of exercise.
  3. For the group & social aspect
    • Every time I’ve traveled I make sure to stop by a Jiu Jitsu / MMA gym for a training session. It is a great way to make friends and can jump start a social group wherever you may be. In many ways you can have a grappling passport.
