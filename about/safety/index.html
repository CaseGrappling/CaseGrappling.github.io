Safety

  • sports, especially combat sports are hard, physically taxing, and can lead to injuries when done with
    • Poor instruction
    • No supervision
    • too much ego
    • not enough respect for your training partners
  • when it comes to grappling sports in particular it is hard to learn in a vacuum. We need training partners to train (that’s why their called training partners!). This means that there must be mutual respect between training partners. We have to take care of eachother not only to advance our technical skill but to be injury free.
  • A common misconception is that all training sessions must be as intense as possible. When I (cade) was training full time alongside members of the DDS, of which there were several world champs / world class grapplers, many of the days they would go very light. Even during live training. You have to gauge your body and some days focusing on techique and “flowing” through positions is better than having a gym war.
  • the most common cause of injury in any sport is falling bodyweight. Brazilian Jiu Jitsu combats this by focusing mainly on ground techniques, referred to as Newaza in early judo. Here it is very hard for a person to fall onto their training partners. However, because our club will be focusing on takedowns aswell it is important that these techniques are done with the upmost of care. Additionally students without wrestling experience and who have not been approved by one of our instructors may NOT practice takedowns in live training.
  • to summarize in order to preserve a safe gym environment we must
    • respect our training partners
    • match the intensity of our training partners during live training (one person can’t be going hard if the other is trying to “flow”)
    • execute standing techniques with the upmost of care
